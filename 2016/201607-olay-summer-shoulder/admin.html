Step 1

Prep your
DÉcollatage

The beauty of the shoulder-baring top lies in its subtlety (no cleavage or midriff exposure here). However, your skin still needs some TLC before making its summer debut. Start in the shower with an exfoliating brush to remove any dull skin. This works best because the bristles are softer and won’t irritate the delicate chest area, unlike harsher salt or apricot scrubs. Next, achieve soft, healthy-looking skin with Olay Ultra Moisture Body Wash. Not all body washes are created equal--case-in-point, this one boasts an entire jar of Olay moisturizers in one bottle. Finally, protect your body with SPF (30 or above, please).

Step 2

Find the Right Fit

Off-the-shoulder tops are nothing new. All you have to do is search images of movie stars from the ’50s for evidence. Think: Lucille Ball, Brigitte Bardot, Natalie Wood and Grace Kelly--the original collarbone queens. But this season, they’ve trickled into the zeitgeist with fresh takes on the vintage silhouette.


Go the classic route with a ruched blouse that drapes every so slightly across your triceps and pair it with a slim pant. Or, opt for the same silhouette in dress form (it works for everything from a garden party to a day at the beach). Not ready to bare it all? Try the shoulder-cutout blouse, which offers a hint of sexiness but the security of spaghetti straps. No matter what style you choose, you’ll be impressed by how quickly these pullovers can romanticize your ensemble.

Step 3

Be Smart About Accessories

When it comes to styling the bare-shouldered look, it’s all about drawing attention up and framing your face. The two key players in this game: the dainty necklace and the dangling earrings. Both jewels work to accent the standout blouse rather than distract from it. To achieve the layered chain look, start with a tighter choker necklace, and then introduce a slightly longer pendant (make sure they’re both the same color metal). And when it comes to earrings, the more playful the better. Minimalism was so last year.

Step 4

Top It Off

The funny thing about the off-the-shoulder top is that when you actually put it on, you become hyper-aware of just how much skin is actually showing. Here’s where your hairstyle comes into play. A tightly wound updo is too harsh for the bohemian top--not to mention, it may cause you look naked. Instead, opt for loose, beachy waves or a low ponytail. Both options compliment the flowy nature of the blouse and truthfully take mere minutes to achieve. (Just watch our clever tutorial.)
