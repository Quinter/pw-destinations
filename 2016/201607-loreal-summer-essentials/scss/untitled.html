Keep your swimsuit from fading

Sun, sand, chlorine: The perfect storm to wrecking a bathing suit. But if you treat your suit before it’s big debut it should make it through the summer, swimmingly.

What to Do:

Prep your suit by soaking it in a vinegar and water mixture (light on the vinegar--one tablespoon will do) for 30 minutes to prevent the dye from bleeding. Note: This works with white bathing suits too keep them from yellowing. Then after each wear, rinse immediately under cold water. Any further washing should be done by hand using a delicate detergent, and definitely don’t ring it out. Instead, roll it in a towel to soak up excess water, then lay flat to dry (out of direct sunlight) to help prevent stretching and fading.
