Dreamy Indulgences Two style setters take us through their perfect day

  • Mary Kate McGgrath Editor In Chief, Purewow

  • Jenny Bernheim Style Blogger, Margo & Me

Purewow’s Editor in Chief, Mary Kate
Be Grath and Style Blogger, Jenny
Bernheim take us through their perfect day.

Morning 8 a.m.

New York City Maialino 2 Lexington Avenue, New York

A cozy and charming take on the classic Italian trattoria, Maialino is exactly the place I want to be at 8 o’clock every morning. Located just off the lobby of the Gramercy Park Hotel, the restaurant is upscale yet not an ounce stuffy. My go-to order is a toss-up between the ricotta pancakes and the cacio e pepe soft scrambled eggs. No diets allowed here.

Eat Big Apple

“Frothy cappuccinos
make the world a
better place.”

- Mary Kate McGrath

Los Angeles Scrumptious Café Bakery 111 N. Hayworth Avenue, West Hollywood

Surprisingly, my dream day begins similarly to my everydaywith a walk to my favorite neighborhood coffee shop, my pup Margo in toe. I’m constantly traveling, so the days when I can savor a stroll through that charming area of West Hollywood are magical. My coffee order also says it all: Scrumptious Latte made with organic espresso and a little hint of cinnamon.

@margoandme

Midday 12 p.m.

New York City The "New" Whitney 99 Gansevoort Street, New York

In its stylish new Renzo Piano building, the Whitney Museum of American Art is an experience that’s as much about the art as it is about the setting. If you go early enough, you can avoid the crowds and take in the Frank Stella Retrospective uninterrupted (FYI: It’s only running until February, so don’t miss out.) The outdoor spaces and balconies with panoramic views are also an unbelievable way to experience the city.

The Wall Street Journal

Los Angeles Cartier Boutique 370 Rodeo Drive, Beverly Hills

I’m a little bit spoiled, as my midday photo shoots normally take place in Beverly Hills (by far the prettiest part of town), where I can pretend to be royalty and pop inside the Cartier Boutique on Rodeo Drive and try on the Classic Diamond bracelet and the Destinée Solitare Ring. Dripping in diamonds can sometimes become a reality, people.

Jenny Bernheim

“Ridiculously luxurious,
but surprisingly
wearable.”

- Jenny Bernheim Amulette De Cartier Earrings($20,300)

Afternoon 3 p.m.

New York City Aire Ancient Baths 88 Franklin Street, New York

To be honest, the best spa experience I’ve ever had was a two-person massage at Surya Spa in L.A. But since that’s Jenny’s territory, I’ll settle for the Aire Ancient Baths. This spa-a subterranean cavern lit by candles alone-features fiery and icy pools, as well as saltwater and even private olive oil baths. The place accommodates only 20 people max, so you’ll never feel overwhelmed.

Aire Ancient Baths

Los Angeles Zinqué Bistro 8684 Melrose Avenue, West Hollywood

Before heading home for some evening festivities, I’ve got to carve out time to catch up on emails and do some work. Luckily, it’s not tough when you love what you do. My favorite spot for this is Zinqué on Melrose. I come for the photogenic patio-with a tiled fireplace and lush garden-butstay for the salmon crème-fraîche tartine and nicoise salad. The perfect afternoon pick-me-up.

@margoandme

Evening 7 p.m.

New York City Cartier At Home Central Park West, New York

When in your life is it appropriate to don diamonds before dinner? On your day of indulgence, that’s when. I absolutely fell in love with this Galanterie de Cartier ring when visiting the 59th Street Cartier Boutique. Its brilliant-cut diamond set in black lacquer makes it the type of piece you just can’t help but flaunt at all night.

Mooseherd

“These are 827
diamonds in this
bracelet. It’s beyond.”

- Mary Kate McGrath Maillon Panthere Bracelet($176,000)

Los Angeles Chateau Marmont 8221 Sunset Boulevard, Los Angeles

Without fail, dinner and drinks ensue at Chateau Marmont. Living just down the street from this exquisite Hollywood landmark makes it hard to pick any other place. The bartender also holds the key to a few cocktail recipes, like the Boulevardier (scotch, vermouth, Campari and a maraschino cherry), that harken back to the days when Marilyn Monroe was a regular and you just might have seen James Dean unwinding after a day on set.

Tory Burch

Late Night 11 p.m.

New York City The Bedford Post Inn 954 Old Post Road, Bedford

Nowadays, escaping the city is my idea of an epic night. I’ll grab an Uber and scurry an hour East to Richard Gere’s quaint hotel in Westchester. In my opinion, a 24-hour getaway has to have three things: Calming surroundings (the inn’s gilded trees are a ringer for New England), at least one diversion (yoga classes held in a beautifully restored barn) and food (farm to table at it’s best).

The Bedford Post Inn

Los Angeles The Soho House 9200 Sunset Boulevard, West Hollywood

While most of my late nights consist of “Club Bed” and “DJ Sheets,” the Soho House is one of my favorite L.A. hangouts. I love that it’s not a nightclub (they actually never have a DJ, and cell phones aren’t even allowed). Instead, people go to mingle, get silly in the massive photo booth and take in the unparalleled views of the city.

The Soho House
